# Sniffout — Demand Validation Report
*Research conducted: March 2026*

---

## Summary Assessment

**There is genuine, documented demand for what Sniffout is building. The POC is worthwhile.**

The research finds a large, active user population (13.5 million dogs, 36% of UK households) with a well-documented set of unmet needs around walk discovery, weather safety, and dog-specific route information. No existing product solves these problems in combination. Users are actively complaining about the gap — in forums, reviews, and surveys — and falling back on Facebook groups as a workaround. Sniffout's specific combination of curated walks + live weather hazard context is not being offered by any competitor.

---

## 1. The Size of the Market

### UK Dog Ownership at Scale
- **13.5 million dogs** are owned in the UK (2024 estimate, up ~1.5 million year-on-year)
- **36% of UK households** own at least one dog
- **28% of UK adults** are dog owners
- Dog ownership grew significantly during COVID and has not reverted

### Walking Behaviour Creates Repeated, Habitual Use
- UK dog owners walk their dogs an average of **6 times per week**
- Average walk duration: **48 minutes**
- Average daily dog care time (dominated by walking): **67 minutes**
- **40% walk 7+ times per week** — this is a daily habit, not an occasional one
- 98% of dog walkers do so because they believe it improves their dog's quality of life

**Implication for Sniffout:** A dog walk discovery and safety app is not a product people open once a month. The walk is a daily ritual. Even if an owner opens the app only once a week to plan something new or check conditions before an unusual outing, the potential touchpoints are enormous.

---

## 2. Evidence of Genuine User Frustration

### "I've Never Found Anything Comprehensive"
The clearest signal of unmet demand comes directly from UK dog owner communities. On a Mumsnet thread specifically asking for apps or websites to help find dog walks:

> *"I've never found anything comprehensive"*

That is a flat statement of market failure — from someone who was actively looking. The thread goes on to identify what they actually need:
- Off-lead area information
- Livestock presence warnings
- Terrain specifics (stiles, road sections)
- Location-specific knowledge

None of this is available in a single mobile product today.

### AllTrails Specifically Called Out
The same thread recommends AllTrails but immediately flags its core failure:

> *"It won't tell you about livestock"*

For UK dog owners, this is a material deficiency. Walking a dog near livestock without knowing it is not just inconvenient — it is a legal issue (livestock worrying laws) and a genuine safety risk. The fact that the most-used trail app in the world fails at this basic UK dog owner need confirms the gap.

### Facebook Groups as the Workaround
When asked where to find the information they need, UK dog owners are repeatedly directed to Facebook groups — specifically *Countryside Dog Walks* on Facebook. One Mumsnet commenter admitted: *"I might have to join Facebook now"* — expressing clear reluctance but acknowledging it as the only option.

**This is a classic pre-product signal.** When users are reluctantly assembling workarounds from unsuitable tools (a social network not designed for the job), there is product opportunity. The need is real; the solution just doesn't exist yet.

### Biscuit App: Privacy for Features
The Mumsnet thread on the Biscuit app (a walk tracker that rewards steps with vouchers) surfaces something interesting: UK dog owners are willing to share detailed personal data — including microchip numbers and precise location history — in exchange for walk features. Privacy concerns were raised, but many users used it anyway. This signals that UK dog owners are not just passive; they will actively adopt technology to improve their walks if the value proposition is clear.

## 3. The Weather Safety Demand Signal

This is the strongest and most quantified demand signal in the research. Multiple independent surveys converge on the same finding: **UK dog owners are walking their dogs in unsafe conditions because they lack the right information at the right moment.**

### The Behaviour Gap
- **Over 80%** of dog owners reduce exercise duration in hot weather, but:
- **43% of UK dog owners have walked their pets in dangerous heat conditions**
- **60% of dog owners do not know that dogs' main sweat glands are located only in their paw pads** — they cannot accurately self-assess heat risk
- More than a quarter of Gen Z dog owners say they are unaware of the warning signs of heat distress

The gap is not lack of concern — **93% of UK dog owners expressed concern about soaring summer temperatures** in one survey. The gap is that concern does not translate to action, because owners lack clear real-time guidance at the moment of deciding whether to go out.

### Winter Walking Behaviour
- **54% of UK dog owners rush their walks in cold or wet weather** — suggesting they are out but cutting walks short reactively
- **43% are less likely to walk in winter** due to restricted daylight hours and conditions
- Dogs are significantly less likely to be exercised in ice (64% of owners) and cold (48%) — but this decision is made on a case-by-case basis with no centralised tool

### The Specific Opportunity
Petplan launched a standalone tool (Weather Furcast) that shows paw-safety traffic light warnings based on temperature. It has no walk discovery attached to it. The fact that a major pet insurer built this as a standalone product confirms there is demand for weather-integrated dog walk guidance — but it has never been combined with actual walk recommendations.

**Sniffout is the only product that asks the compound question: "Is it safe to go out, and if so, where should I go?"**

## 4. The Route Discovery Demand Signal

### Dogs (and Owners) Get Bored
A Mumsnet thread asking whether owners walk the same route consistently attracted strong community consensus: **variety in routes is actively desired** for dogs' mental stimulation. Key sentiments:

- Dogs show *"extra spring in their step"* on new routes
- Users rotate between 3–10 different walks to maintain engagement
- One commenter expressed empathy for dogs that *"go literally in a square around the block three times a day"*
- Dogs Trust's official recommendation: *"vary walking routes to provide environmental enrichment"*

The aspiration for varied, new walks is established. The limiting factor is discovery — owners don't know where to go.

### The Technology Adoption Trend
Ordnance Survey reported a significant uptick in use of their mapping app specifically for discovering **urban green spaces, parks, and footpaths** — owners actively seeking new routes. The appetite is there; the tools are general-purpose and not dog-specific.

### Walkiees Proxy Validation
Walkiees, a UK-specific dog walk discovery website, has **50,000+ registered members** and has operated for over a decade. This is not a large product, but it proves a persistent, ongoing user base willing to seek out and use a dedicated dog walk discovery resource. It also proves the concept is not a fad — the platform has sustained interest for 10+ years.

- Active threads asking for walk-finding resources
- Explicit statements of no comprehensive solution existing
- Users reluctantly pivoting to Facebook as best available workaround
- Specific feature asks: livestock warnings, off-lead info, terrain details

- Weather significantly affects walk decisions for the majority of owners
- Large knowledge gaps around heat safety — owners want to make good decisions but lack tools

### Academic Research
- A 2021 study in *Animals* (PMC8614552) surveying 3,153 UK dog owners found weather is a top decision-making variable for walks, and recommends *"evidence-based owner education campaigns"* — Sniffout's in-app weather hazard warnings directly address this recommendation
- UK dog owners are motivated walkers but their decision-making suffers from information deficits

### App Store & Product Signals
- Walkiees: 50,000+ registered users, 10+ years, proving sustained demand
- Dog Walks (Countryside Books): Paid subscription with active user base, openly acknowledges regional coverage gaps
- PlayDogs: 170k downloads but almost entirely in France/Switzerland — UK market effectively untapped
- Biscuit app: Users accept friction and privacy trade-offs for walk-related features, showing willingness to engage with walk tech

## 7. Honest Assessment

### Is there a real gap?
**Yes.** The market has several apps but they each solve one part of the problem: AllTrails for general trail discovery, Walkiees for UK community walks, Weather Furcast for paw heat safety. No product connects walk discovery to real-time weather safety context in a mobile-first, no-login UK experience. That gap is real and is being felt by users right now.

### Is there genuine demand?
**Yes.** The research shows:
1. A massive, habitual user base (millions of UK dog owners walking daily)
2. Active, documented frustration with the absence of comprehensive tools
3. Specific feature requests that map directly to Sniffout's feature set
4. Willingness to engage with technology (Biscuit, Walkiees memberships, mapping app uptake)
5. A clear information deficit around weather safety that has health consequences

The demand is not speculative. It is expressed in forum threads, evidenced by academic research, and quantified in national surveys.

### Is Sniffout well-positioned to fill it?
**Yes, with caveats.**

**Strengths of the current positioning:**
- The combination of curated walks + weather is genuinely unique
- No login is a meaningful differentiator — lower friction than every competitor
- Curated content means it works from day one everywhere in the UK, unlike PlayDogs
- The weather hazard detection (rain/heat/wind) maps directly to the documented safety knowledge gap
- PWA means no app store friction; instant access from a link

**The honest caveat:**
Sniffout's current WALKS_DB covers 50+ walks. Walkiees has 1,400+. Dog Walks (Countryside Books) has 500+ for England alone. For many users in many regions, the first question will be whether their local area is covered. The AI-generated quality is high (founder-verified) but the volume will need to grow to make the walk discovery side feel comprehensive. This is a solvable problem — the database can be expanded — but it is the most significant gap between current state and compelling utility for the majority of UK dog owners.

**The core thesis is validated:** Build the weather + walk combination, keep it free and frictionless, and expand coverage. That is a product that has a real reason to exist and no current competitor.
