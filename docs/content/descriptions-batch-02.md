WALK ID: roseberry-topping
PERSONA: Morag
USERNAME: morag83
DESCRIPTION: That distinctive wee peak is properly satisfying to climb - not a big day out but the views from the top over the Cleveland Plain are grand on a clear morning. Underfoot it's mixed - good path through Newton Wood but rockier as you get higher, and there can be sheep on the lower pasture so you'll need the lead handy. Midge gets off-lead on the moorland section and loves it. Best in autumn when the bracken turns; there's a decent pub in Newton under Roseberry at the bottom if you time it right.

WALK ID: dalby-forest
PERSONA: Deborah
USERNAME: DeborahH
DESCRIPTION: A really lovely woodland walk with well-marked paths throughout - the Woodcock Way Circular is perfectly manageable for dogs of all ages, and Biscuit and Marmalade can be off-lead the whole way as there's no livestock in the forest. Do bear in mind there's a toll road charge to reach the visitor centre (around £5-£9 depending on season), but the facilities are excellent once you're there. The visitor centre has everything you'd need for a proper day out, and there's a café on site which Biscuit and Marmalade are very familiar with by now.

WALK ID: beacon-fell
PERSONA: Pete
USERNAME: PeteR63
DESCRIPTION: An underwalked Forest of Bowland AONB fell circuit using a mix of public footpath and permissive path across Lancashire County Council-managed land. Dogs can be off-lead for most of it, though sheep do graze the fell at certain times of year - the council website doesn't confirm exact dates so assume livestock may be present and keep Scratchy close on the upper grassland. The drainage is notably good for a moorland site - dont expect the boot-soaking bogs you get elsewhere in the Pennines.

WALK ID: rivington-pike
PERSONA: Jamie
USERNAME: jamieok
DESCRIPTION: Honestly one of the better ones in Greater Manchester's orbit - the old tower at the top is a proper landmark and the views back over the reservoir are worth the climb. Ghost can run freely on most of the open moor sections but you'll hit sheep near the top so watch it. Gets very busy on sunny weekends, go early or it's a nightmare for parking even though the car park itself is free. A few stiles on some variants but nothing terrible.

WALK ID: lyme-park
PERSONA: Pete
USERNAME: PeteR63
DESCRIPTION: The NT dog walking map (downloadable PDF from its website) is essential here - the off-lead areas are not obvious on the ground and getting it wrong means walking into the red and fallow deer enclosures or the Highland cattle section, neither of which you want with a dog. Knightslow Wood is off-lead year-round and is the main draw for dog walkers; the wider parkland and moorland beyond the wood are excellent but require a lead near all stock. A 5-mile circuit taking in the moorland above the hall gives exceptional views toward Kinder Scout on a clear day. Parking at the main NT car park off the A6 near Disley.

WALK ID: bradgate-park
PERSONA: Priya
USERNAME: Priya&Pretzel
DESCRIPTION: The ruins of Lady Jane Grey's house rising out of bracken-covered hillside are a genuinely unexpected sight - we came expecting a country park and got something much more atmospheric, and Pretzel was completely obsessed with the rocks around Old John Tower. It's a big open park so there's plenty of room even on busy weekends, though the red deer roam freely so keep an eye out and pop the lead on when they're close. Parking is paid at Newtown Linford but the park itself is free which feels fair for what you get. Deffo one to go back to.

WALK ID: calke-abbey
PERSONA: Pete
USERNAME: PeteR63
DESCRIPTION: Worth understanding the access layers before you go - the wider parkland is open to all, but entry to the house and walled garden requires NT membership. Dogs are permitted off lead in the parkland and woodland under close control, which in practice means keeping a reasonable eye on them given the deer, cattle and sheep across the meadows and along the historic lime avenue. The estate has a pleasantly forgotten quality about it - the walled kitchen garden in a state of deliberate decay, the peeling outbuildings - that makes it feel like a different sort of NT property. Some stiles and uneven ground throughout; the 4-mile circuit is well worth the time.

PERSONA: Jamie
USERNAME: jamieok

WALK ID: symonds-yat
PERSONA: Jamie
USERNAME: jamieok
DESCRIPTION: The hand-pulled ferry crossing the Wye is what makes this one - it's a tiny bit of a faff, you have to shout across for the ferryman, but Ghost thought it was brilliant and honestly so did I. WALK ID: penllergare-valley-woods
PERSONA: Priya
USERNAME: Priya&Pretzel
DESCRIPTION: A Victorian woodland estate with a waterfall that photographs beautifully, all tucked just off the M4 near Swansea - genuinely one of the better surprises we've had looking for somewhere new. Pretzel managed the paths fine, they're well maintained and not too steep, though it can get muddy after rain so worth checking conditions. Dogs need to be on leads during nesting season (March to August) which is worth knowing. There's a free parking option nearby if you don't want to pay the main car park fee.

WALK ID: llansteffan-castle
PERSONA: Priya
USERNAME: Priya&Pretzel
DESCRIPTION: Wide sandy beach, a ruined Norman castle on the clifftop, and estuary views that stretch for miles - we genuinely couldn't believe how much one circular route fits in, and Pretzel absolutely loved it. The circular route out to Wharley Point is easy going with no stiles and dogs are welcome on the beach year-round which is such a bonus. Pretzel handled the cliff path sections absolutely fine, the surface is good. Parking on The Green is free out of season which makes it an easy yes.

WALK ID: sheringham-park
PERSONA: Deborah
USERNAME: DeborahH
DESCRIPTION: A wonderful National Trust property with trails suitable for all ages - the rhododendrons in May are quite spectacular, and the path to the clifftop viewpoint over the sea is well worth including on the route. Do bear in mind dogs must be on lead between March and July due to nesting and the cattle that graze the parkland, which is perfectly reasonable. The NT provides drinking bowls near the visitor centre which is always a bonus, and the car park is at Upper Sheringham with pay and display.

WALK ID: tehidy-country-park
PERSONA: Priya
USERNAME: Priya&Pretzel
DESCRIPTION: Cornwall's biggest woodland and it's completely free to park at multiple entrances - we went in via East Drive and it was a lovely easy loop around the lake with Pretzel off-lead the whole way as there's no livestock anywhere on site. The café by the lake does good coffee and was dog-friendly when we visited. Perfect for smaller dogs - the paths are level and well-kept and Pretzel's little legs handled it with no trouble at all. Deffo worth bookmarking for a low-effort day - not a showstopper but genuinely lovely for what it is.

WALK ID: moors-valley
PERSONA: Deborah
USERNAME: DeborahH
DESCRIPTION: One of the most well-organised dog-friendly country parks I've come across - there are two designated dog paddling areas which the boys absolutely love, and the long forest walk covers a lovely 5 miles through varied woodland without feeling repetitive. Dogs are off-lead in most of the forest, though you'll want leads near the visitor centre, lake and picnic areas where it gets busy at weekends. Parking is charged but pay on exit, and the facilities are genuinely excellent. It's one of those places where you can happily spend an entire morning without covering the same ground twice.

WALK ID: kinnoull-hill
PERSONA: Morag
USERNAME: morag83
DESCRIPTION: Right on the edge of Perth and you'd barely know the city was there - the woodland paths climb steadily to the cliff edge above the Tay and the views are braw on a clear day, the river winding south through patchwork fields below the ruined folly tower. Midge can be off-lead throughout which is rare to say for a walk this close to a city centre. Paths are a mix of wide gravel tracks and narrower natural routes - some muddy sections in wet weather, mind. Free parking at Jubilee Car Park on Dundee Road; there's a good café in Kinnoull village a short walk from the car park if you need something warm after.

WALK ID: elterwater-langdale
PERSONA: Deborah
USERNAME: DeborahH
DESCRIPTION: A gentle loop through the Great Langdale valley with the sound of the river beside you for most of it - the path past Skelwith Force is well-marked and the classic Lakeland backdrop never feels like a postcard because you're properly in it. There are cattle and sheep in the valley fields so you'll want the lead on in those sections, and a few stiles to navigate which is worth knowing if your dog finds them difficult. The National Trust car park in the village is pay and display but perfectly convenient. There's a dog-friendly pub in Elterwater village for afterwards, which Biscuit and Marmalade consider a non-negotiable part of the route.

WALK ID: newtondale-levisham
PERSONA: Pete
USERNAME: PeteR63
DESCRIPTION: One of the North York Moors' genuinely undervisited routes - start at the small car park by Levisham Station (grid ref SE 818 910) where there's an honesty box, and the path drops you immediately into the glacial valley which is dramatic from the first steps. The Forestry England woodland sections are open access throughout and Scratchy can range freely; the one section to watch is near Kale Pot Hole Farm where livestock are present and the path becomes a public right of way through a farmyard - keep dogs under close control there. No stiles, just gates, and the full 6-mile circuit is excellent underfoot in dry conditions.

WALK ID: loch-ard-aberfoyle
PERSONA: Morag
USERNAME: morag83
DESCRIPTION: Forestry and Land Scotland land throughout, so Midge has the run of the whole route without restriction - no livestock, no lead requirements, just proper forest walking with loch views opening up through the trees as you follow the Loch Ard shore. The waymarking is decent enough, though the forest tracks all look similar so a quick glance at the map before you go is sensible. Your best in late spring or early autumn when the midges are manageable. Free parking on the B829 just west of Aberfoyle; the Forth Inn in the village does food and is worth timing the walk around.

WALK ID: rhossili-down
PERSONA: Morag
USERNAME: morag83
DESCRIPTION: The Down sits above the famous bay and most people never bother climbing it, which is a shame because the ridge walk gives you three bays at once - Rhossili, Llangennith and the Broughton Bay stretch to the north, all laid out below you. It's open National Trust moorland so Midge gets plenty of freedom, though horses and cattle do graze the Down so you'll need to stay alert and have the lead ready. Underfoot it's short cropped grass and rougher sections - fine in dry conditions but can be slippery if wet; there's an NT café in the village at the start if you need a warm-up before heading up. Mind the wind up on the ridge, it can be properly fierce in any season.
