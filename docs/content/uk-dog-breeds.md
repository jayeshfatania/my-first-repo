60 most common UK dog breeds plus 3 special options, for use in the Sniffout dog profile selector.

Ordered by UK popularity (KC registration data, PDSA PAW Report, Dogs Trust studies 2022–2024). Crossbreeds are included as they account for a large proportion of the UK dog population.

Plain names used throughout. "Labrador" not "Labrador Retriever", etc.

## Notes

- **Cockapoo** (Cocker x Poodle): the most popular crossbreed in the UK by a significant margin. Included at position 3.
- **Labradoodle** (Labrador x Poodle): second most popular crossbreed. Position 6.
- **Cavapoo** (Cavalier x Poodle): third most popular crossbreed. Position 9.
- **Goldendoodle** (Golden Retriever x Poodle): fourth most popular crossbreed. Position 19.
- **Cavachon** (Cavalier x Bichon Frise): increasingly popular. Position 40.
- **Maltipoo** (Maltese x Poodle): popular smaller crossbreed. Position 41.
- **Sprocker** (Springer x Cocker): common working/pet crossbreed. Position 56.
- **Schnoodle** (Schnauzer x Poodle): growing in popularity. Position 57.
- **Lurcher**: included as a category because lurchers are extremely common in the UK, especially in rural and working dog communities. Technically a crossbreed but widely self-identified as a breed by owners.
- **Dachshund**: one of the fastest-growing KC registrations in the UK over 2020–2024. Miniature and Standard included under a single entry as most owners simply say "Dachshund".
- **Staffordshire Bull Terrier**: the most common dog in UK rescue centres; very high ownership in urban areas.
- **Vizsla**: rapid growth in UK popularity since 2015; strongly associated with active/outdoorsy owners — a key Sniffout user type.
- **Dobermann**: UK spelling used (not "Doberman").
- **Westie**: common shorthand for West Highland White Terrier; most users will search for "Westie".

Total: 60 breeds + 3 special options = 63 dropdown entries.
