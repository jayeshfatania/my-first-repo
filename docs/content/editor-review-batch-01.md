# Editor Review — Batch 01
**Batch scope:** 46 walk descriptions (26 existing, 20 new)

## 1. BATCH OVERVIEW

The batch is broadly solid. The persona voices are distinct and consistent — Deborah's warmth, Jamie's dry directness, Morag's dry-but-affectionate Scottish register, Pete's forensic formality, and Priya's enthusiastic first-person storytelling all land convincingly. The imperfections are dialled in well: Pete drops apostrophes, Priya swaps "its" and "it's", Morag uses fragments and ellipses. The content itself is accurate and specific — real landmarks, real terrain conditions, genuine practical detail. This is the foundation the product needs.

However, there are three systemic problems that need addressing before this batch is validator-ready:

1. **Walk-name openers.** The most common violation of the brief. Descriptions must not open with the walk name or location name. It appears across all five personas, concentrated more heavily in the new-walk batch than the existing-walk batch. Pete is the most frequent offender.

2. **Priya's "We loved/love this one" opener.** Used three times across the full batch (wimbledon-common, st-marthas-hill, bute-park-cardiff). Once is a quirk. Twice is a pattern. Three times is a formula. Two of the three need reworking.

3. **Deborah's "which is always a bonus" sign-off.** Present in 7 of her 9 entries. The persona brief specifies this as a quirk — an occasional habit, not a closer applied mechanically to every description. It needs to appear in no more than 3–4 entries across the full Deborah set, which means removing it from at least three and finding another way to close those.

A smaller secondary issue: several descriptions run to five sentences, which is one over the 2–4 sentence guideline. This is not always a problem — some walk descriptions need that fifth beat — but it should be flagged case by case.

The existing-walk batch is noticeably tighter than the new-walk batch. The new-walk batch has more walk-name openers and slightly looser construction. This is consistent with what you'd expect from a second production run without a correction pass.
