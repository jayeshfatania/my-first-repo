## Tone Benchmark

Sniffout should sound like a friend who knows dogs, knows the outdoors, and actually cares. Not a weather service. Not a directory. A companion — one who notices that you probably want to know right now whether it's worth pulling on your wellies, not after reading three paragraphs of hedged caveats.

The benchmark is Monzo or Citymapper: clear, warm, occasionally dry, never gushing. Gets to the point. Treats the user as an adult. Occasionally charming. Consistent throughout.

**Key principle established by PO:** Weather intelligence is Sniffout's competitive differentiator — but it's the engine, not the product. The product is the walk experience and the companion relationship. Weather earns its prominence once the user is inside the app; the brand positioning is about discovery and companionship. All weather-specific copy (verdict strings, hazard titles, paw safety, walk window) remains prominently placed — it just doesn't lead the headline hierarchy or the tagline.

## 🔁 Tagline — Revised

**PO feedback:** "Every walk, safely." was returned. It leads with safety/weather when the product is fundamentally about walk discovery and companionship. The weather intelligence is the engine; the positioning should be the experience.

**PO brief:**
- About the dog walking experience — discovery, finding new routes, the walk itself
- Feels like a companion: personal, warm, quietly confident
- Works without any weather reference
- Distinctive enough that it couldn't belong to a generic walking app

**Revised primary recommendation:**
> **Dog walks, done properly.**

Quietly confident. British in register. Implies a standard has been applied — that the alternatives haven't done it right. Couldn't belong to a generic walking app (it names dogs explicitly). Doesn't need weather to make its claim. Works on a home screen, a share card, social posts.

**Alternatives for consideration:**

> **The walk app made for dog owners.**
> *(Functional, specific — less distinctive but very clear)*

> **Walks your dog will love.**
> *(Warm, dog-first — but slightly soft as a brand line)*

> **Go further. Together.**
> *(Companionship angle — but "together" risks implying a social feature that doesn't exist yet)*

> **Find the walks worth finding.**
> *(Discovery-focused — confident and specific, though less dog-centric)*

**Home screen subheadline — Revised** ⏳
Previous: `Great walks. Safe conditions. No fuss.` — returned by PO (still leads with safety).

**Revised:** `Handpicked walks. Live conditions. No account.`

Three clean beats: the product (curation), the differentiator (live conditions — present but not leading), the trust signal (no account). Shorter. Each word earns its place.

**Alternative:** `50+ walks, chosen for you. Free. No sign-up.`

## PO Pushbacks — Applied in This Revision

**1. "Effort" vs "Difficulty" — keep "Difficulty"**
The reviewer suggested renaming the walk detail label from "Difficulty" to "Effort." PO overruled: "Difficulty" is the industry standard across AllTrails, OS Maps, and every walk app in the competitive set. Users scan it immediately. "Effort" introduces ambiguity ("effort for who — me or my dog?"). Reverted.

**2. "No ads" — do not include**
The punchy social proof option `No log-in, no ads, no nonsense` was removed. "No ads" is a permanent product promise that hasn't been made. At POC stage, ruling out ad-based monetisation is premature. Use `No account needed` instead. If a no-ads decision is confirmed later, this line can be added.

**3. "You found it." onboarding title — rejected**
Too oblique for a first-time user who doesn't yet understand what they've found. Replaced with `Good walks for you and your dog.`

**4. Paw emoji frequency — restrict to paw safety section**
🐾 is used far too liberally in the current app. In v2, reserve it for the paw safety section specifically. Not on: loading states, contribution banners, share buttons, submit forms, or onboarding cards. Approved copy strings in this document have been updated to reflect this.

**5. Em dashes throughout**
All dashes in copy strings should be em dashes (—), not hyphens (-) or en dashes (–). Developer to do a search-and-replace pass when implementing.
