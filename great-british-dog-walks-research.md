> **Prepared for:** Sniffout product team

> **Researcher note:** Instagram follower count confirmed at 137,000. TikTok and Facebook group counts remain unverified.

## Executive Summary

Great British Dog Walks (GBDW) is not a direct competitor to Sniffout. It is a personal lifestyle content brand — editorial and inspirational in nature — run by a solo creator named Emily Florence Gill. It has no app, no maps, no structured route data, and no plans to build a utility product. The competitive overlap is thematic (dog walks in the UK) but not functional.

The more interesting finding is the **partnership opportunity**. GBDW sits at the top of the funnel (inspiration and discovery via social media and blog) while Sniffout sits at the middle and bottom (planning and execution). They serve the same user at different moments in the same journey. That gap is exactly where a partnership creates mutual value.

**Recommendation: low competitive threat, high partnership potential. Pursue outreach.**

## 1. What They Are

### The brand

Great British Dog Walks is a lifestyle blog and social media presence run by **Emily Florence Gill** and her Jack Russell Terrier, **Murphy**. The tagline is "Helping you find the best dog friendly places to visit in the UK." The brand covers dog walks, accommodation, dining, activities, and gear — framed as personal first-person adventure journalism rather than structured route information.

It is a **personal creator brand**, not a platform or product company. Emily operates solo, or very nearly so. The brand launched no earlier than 2018 (the earliest blog content found) and has grown steadily without a significant funding event or team expansion.

## 2. Competitive Analysis

### Where they overlap with Sniffout

| Dimension | Great British Dog Walks | Sniffout |
|-----------|------------------------|----------|
| Walk discovery | Yes — inspirational, editorial | Yes — functional, curated |
| UK focus | Yes | Yes |
| Dog-specific content | Yes | Yes |
| Live weather | No | Yes |
| Interactive maps | No | Yes |
| Structured route data | No | Yes |
| Dog-friendly filters | Partial (mentions off-lead areas in prose) | Yes |
| App / PWA | No | Yes |
| No login required | N/A | Yes |
| Community | Facebook group | Not yet |
| Social media presence | Yes — personality-led | No |
| Physical product (book) | Yes | No |
| Monetised brand deals | Yes | Not yet |

### Where Sniffout is ahead

- **Utility over inspiration.** Sniffout tells you whether to go today, based on live conditions. GBDW tells you about places you might want to go eventually.
- **Live weather integration.** No UK walk discovery brand combines weather hazard context with walk recommendations in the way Sniffout does. This is a genuine differentiator with no equivalent in GBDW.
- **Structured, filterable walk data.** Off-lead status, livestock, terrain, stiles, parking — none of this exists in GBDW content. Sniffout provides it at the walk level.
- **Speed of access.** No login, no account, open immediately on mobile. GBDW requires navigating a blog or scrolling social feeds.
- **App-first experience.** Sniffout works as a PWA with offline capability. GBDW is a website designed for desktop browsing.

### Where GBDW is ahead

- **Social reach and distribution.** GBDW has an active social audience who engage with walk content. Sniffout has no distribution channel yet.
- **Brand personality.** Emily and Murphy are a recognisable face. Sniffout is currently a product without a personality.
- **Content depth per destination.** Blog posts run 800-1,500 words with multiple photos, accommodation tips, and dining recommendations — a richer experience for trip planning than a walk card.
- **Physical product.** A published book provides credibility, press coverage, and shelf presence that a PWA cannot match.
- **Community.** The Facebook group creates ongoing engagement and word of mouth. Sniffout has no equivalent.

### Direct competitor or different need?

**Different need.** GBDW satisfies inspiration and aspiration — "where could I take Murphy on our next weekend away?" Sniffout satisfies decision-making and execution — "should I go to the woods today, and which walk suits the conditions?" A user could realistically use both in the same weekend: GBDW on Thursday to pick a destination, Sniffout on Saturday morning to check the weather and choose the route.

They are complementary, not competitive.

## 3. Partnership Opportunities

The case for a partnership is strong, and the mechanics are straightforward. Emily actively seeks brand partnerships via a media kit, has an established audience of exactly the right demographic, and produces content in the format (warm, personal, dog-focused walk descriptions) that Sniffout needs.

### Option A — Content collaboration

Emily's editorial style is very close to the persona-based descriptions Sniffout is already building (see descriptions-batch-03.md for reference). A formal content partnership could see Emily contribute 5-10 walk descriptions per quarter, credited as "curated by Great British Dog Walks," providing both authentic content and a co-marketing arrangement.

**What Emily gets:** In-app attribution, link to her website and book, exposure to Sniffout users.
**What Sniffout gets:** High-quality editorial content from a trusted voice, implied endorsement from a known brand in the space.

### Option B — Social promotion

Emily promotes Sniffout to her audience as her go-to walk planning app — fitting naturally alongside her existing tool recommendations (she already references OS Maps in her content). 2-4 posts per quarter on Instagram and TikTok, showing how she uses Sniffout to plan walks from the book.

**What Emily gets:** Paid brand partnership fee (modest, appropriate to her tier).
**What Sniffout gets:** Distribution to a warm, relevant audience of UK dog owners who are already primed to discover walk apps.

### Option C — Book cross-promotion

Sniffout features a curated collection of walks from *Follow My Lead*, attributed to Emily with a link to buy the book. The in-app collection reads: "Walks from Emily Gill's Follow My Lead — available from all good bookshops."

**What Emily gets:** Digital exposure for the book beyond traditional book marketing channels.
**What Sniffout gets:** Instant credibility of curated content from a published author, press-ready partnership story.

### Option D — Ambassador model

Emily becomes a named Sniffout ambassador and "Head of Walks" — her name and Murphy's face appear in the app's editorial layer. She commits to a quarterly content drop (walks + descriptions) and a social mention each time. Sniffout is mentioned in future book editions or blog posts as Emily's recommended planning tool.

**Risk:** Ties Sniffout's brand identity to a single creator. If Emily's brand has any controversy or pivots direction, it affects Sniffout. Manage with a clear, limited-scope agreement.

### Ideal partnership — recommendation

Start with **Option B** (a paid social post or two) to test audience response, then layer in **Option A** (content) if the audience response is positive. Keep the initial arrangement low-commitment on both sides. Emily's contact is greatbritishdogwalks@gmail.com and she has a media kit ready — this is a standard outreach pitch, not a complex negotiation.

**Do not** lead with Option D (ambassador model) at this stage — reserve that for post-launch when Sniffout has an established user base.

## 4. Growth Ideas for Sniffout

### Seasonal content strategy

GBDW's most topically resonant content is seasonal — bluebell season articles, spring walk roundups, Christmas dog-friendly guide. Sniffout could build a seasonal walk collections layer: "Best walks this week," "Bluebell season — where to go," "Coastal walks for summer." This is content GBDW creates editorially but cannot deliver as a live, filterable feature. Sniffout can.

### Dog-and-family angle

Emily's shift toward dog-and-baby content reflects a real user segment: young families with dogs who need walks accessible to buggies and toddlers. Sniffout removed `isPushchairFriendly` from the schema — this may be worth reconsidering if there is product-market fit signal here. Even a "suitable for families" tag at the walk level could serve this audience without requiring a new schema field formally.

### No-friction access as a marketing message

Emily's audience is casual walkers and weekend explorers, not power users. Sniffout's no-login approach is a direct appeal to this demographic and should be messaged explicitly in any partnership content: "Open it and go — no account needed."

### Community walk recommendations

Emily's Facebook group is people recommending dog-friendly places to each other — exactly the behaviour Sniffout would want to capture inside the app. Community features are deferred per CLAUDE.md, but this validates that the demand is real. When the community tab is eventually built, GBDW's group model is a useful reference.

### Weather as a content hook

Emily references checking weather before walks but uses separate tools. Sniffout's weather intelligence is a genuine answer to a friction she describes in her content. Any partnership post should lead with this: "I used to check three apps before a walk — now Sniffout tells me everything in one."

## 5. Risk Assessment

### Could GBDW launch an app and become a direct competitor?

**Risk: Very low.**

Emily has no technical background, no development team, no tech investors, and no signals of pivoting to product. She has just published a book through a traditional publisher — her trajectory is firmly editorial. The most realistic version of this risk is GBDW being acquired by or partnering with an existing platform (e.g. DogFriendly.co.uk) that then builds a walk discovery layer. Even that scenario is speculative and distant.

### Brand or IP clash?

**Risk: Negligible.**

The names "Sniffout" and "Great British Dog Walks" do not conflict. Brand identities are completely distinct — one is a utility product, one is a personality-led lifestyle brand. No trademark concerns identified.

### Dependency risk if partnership is pursued

**Risk: Low-medium if poorly structured.**

If Sniffout relies heavily on Emily as a content source and she deprioritises the relationship (creator brands are volatile; life events, burnout, and pivots are common), content supply could be interrupted. Mitigate by treating GBDW as a supplementary content source, not the primary one, and ensuring any agreement includes a clear exit clause.

### Audience expectation mismatch

**Risk: Low.**

GBDW's audience expects inspirational lifestyle content. A Sniffout promotion that over-indexes on technical features (maps, filters, data) could feel off-brand on her channels. Any promoted content must be framed in Emily's authentic voice — "I used this to plan our walk" — not as a product feature list.
